UNESCO World Heritage Sites in India

Explore the rich cultural and natural heritage of India

Cultural Heritage

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, is an ivory-white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Ajanta Caves (Maharashtra)

A group of 30 rock-cut Buddhist caves famous for their beautiful frescoes and sculptures. They date from the 2nd century BCE to about 480 CE and illustrate the evolution of Indian art.

Ellora Caves (Maharashtra)

Comprising 34 caves, Ellora features Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain temples carved into rock. The Kailasa Temple is a remarkable example of rock-cut architecture and artistry.

Red Fort (Delhi)

A fortification in Delhi built by Shah Jahan, known for its massive red sandstone walls. It served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors and is a symbol of Indiaâ€™s sovereignty.

Hampi (Karnataka)

The ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire, known for their impressive temples and palaces.A UNESCO site for its rich historical context and stunning landscape dotted with boulders and rice fields.

Khajuraho Temples (Madhya Pradesh)

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, is an ivory-white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Sun Temple, Konark (Odisha)

A 13th-century temple dedicated to the sun god, Surya, designed as a gigantic chariot. Known for its exquisite stone carvings and architectural brilliance.

Group of Monuments at Mahabalipuram (Tamil Nadu)

Famous for its rock-cut temples and monolithic sculptures, including the Shore Temple. These monuments illustrate the art and architecture of the Pallava dynasty.

Jantar Mantar (Jaipur and Delhi)

Astronomical observatories featuring a collection of architectural astronomical instruments. Built in the 18th century, they reflect the scientific advancements of that era.

Qutub Minar (Delhi)

A towering minaret made of red sandstone and marble, standing at 73 meters. Built in the 12th century, it is a prime example of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Natural Heritage

Kaziranga National Park

Kaziranga National Park, located in Assam, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its population of the Indian one-horned rhinoceros and diverse wildlife.

Sundarbans National Park

Sundarbans National Park, located in West Bengal, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the largest tidal halophytic mangrove forest in the world.

Nanda Devi and Valley of Flowers National Parks (Uttarakhand)

Known for their stunning landscapes and diverse flora, particularly alpine flowers. The area is sacred and holds significant cultural importance, as well as ecological value.

Mixed Heritage (Cultural and Natural)

Khangchendzonga National Park

Khangchendzonga National Park, located in Sikkim, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its unique biodiversity and cultural significance.
